Protect our Kauri trees 🌲

Kauri trees are some of the oldest living organisms on Earth. Many are over 1,000 years old, and some giants like Tāne Mahuta have stood for nearly two millennia.

They once covered huge areas of the upper North Island, forming dense forests that supported birds, insects, fungi, and entire ecosystems.

Why Kauri Trees Matter

Kauri trees are a taonga (treasure) of Aotearoa. Their massive trunks and towering height make them one of the most iconic species in New Zealand. Kauri forests store huge amounts of carbon, help regulate water flow, and create unique acidic soil environments that support rare plants and fungi.

Kaitiakitanga — Guardianship

Kaitiakitanga is the Māori concept of caring for the natural world. It’s about protecting what is precious for future generations. Kauri have deep cultural significance, representing strength, resilience, and connection to the land.

Our Responsibility

Protecting Kauri is about preserving culture, history, and the natural identity of Aotearoa.
